# Operator guide — LAO

Portal: no self-service e-procurement portal -- proposals go through
the One-stop Investment Service Office (OISO). For a Controlled Business
List activity, an investor first registers an enterprise with the
Department of Enterprise Management (Ministry of Industry and Commerce),
then applies for an investment license via the Central OISO (Investment
Promotion Department, Ministry of Planning and Investment) or Provincial
OISO (Provincial Planning and Investment Department), decided within 25
working days (Art.36). For a business not on the Controlled List,
enterprise registration alone (Law on Enterprise) suffices, with an
optional investment-promotion certificate for promoted sectors. For a
concession business (land concession, SEZ development, mining, energy,
aviation/telecom, PPP), the operator submits an MOU/feasibility-study
application via the Central or Provincial OISO, decided within 65
working days (Art.46), followed by a concession agreement signed by the
Government or the Provincial Administration Committee -- EXCEPT for a
narrow set of high-impact criteria (land concessions of 10,000 hectares
or more, or relocations of more than 500 households) that Art.49 routes
to the National Assembly instead, and relocations of fewer than 100
households that Art.50 routes to the Provincial People's Assembly.
Filings that claim the wrong legislative-body tier for their own
declared land-area/relocated-household figures clear the Arts.49-50
authority-jurisdiction gate before a filing can proceed. Entity: the
Department of Enterprise Management's Enterprise Registration
Certificate, which bundles the Tax Identification Number. Human-gated
filing only.
